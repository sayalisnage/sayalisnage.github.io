About Me

I am a data science professional with a background in engineering and a growing passion for building AI-driven solutions that deliver meaningful social and environmental impact. I bring a unique blend of technical rigor, cross-domain understanding, and a strong interest in applied machine learning to solve real-world problems.

My early career in the electrical and power sector honed my skills in system-level thinking, data analysis, and project management. As I transitioned into data science, I developed expertise in Python programming, statistical modeling with R, time series forecasting, data visualization, and end-to-end machine learning workflows. I particularly enjoy working on projects that intersect public services, sustainability, and intelligent automation.

I’ve worked with a variety of datasets—ranging from environmental and energy data to public service and child care platforms—and have experience turning raw data into actionable tools using frameworks like Streamlit, scikit-learn, and OpenAI’s GPT models. I take pride in crafting solutions that are not just accurate, but also interpretable and user-friendly.

What drives me is the potential of data science to enable smarter decisions, uncover hidden patterns, and contribute to a more equitable future. I'm particularly interested in responsible AI, interpretable models, and AI applications in sustainability, education, and smart cities.

Outside of building models and dashboards, I enjoy exploring new technologies, contributing to open-source projects, and learning from other data professionals. I’m always open to collaboration and conversations that challenge conventional thinking and push the boundaries of what data can do.
